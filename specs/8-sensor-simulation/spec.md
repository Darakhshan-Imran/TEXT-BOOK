### SR3: Key Topics Overview (150-200 words)
**Camera Simulation**: Simulated cameras provide realistic visual data with configurable parameters like resolution, field of view, and noise characteristics. These simulations help test computer vision algorithms before deployment on real hardware.

**LIDAR and Range Sensor Simulation**: LIDAR simulation models the physics of light reflection to generate accurate depth and distance measurements. This includes simulating beam divergence, reflections, and environmental factors that affect real sensors.

**Inertial Sensor Simulation**: IMU and other inertial sensor simulation includes modeling noise, drift, and environmental factors that affect real sensors. This allows for testing navigation and control algorithms with realistic sensor limitations.

### SR7: Why This Matters (75-100 words)
Sensor simulation is crucial because real sensors are expensive, can be damaged during testing, and may not be available during early development phases. Simulation allows for testing edge cases, failure modes, and environmental conditions that would be difficult or dangerous to reproduce with real hardware, accelerating development cycles.
