### SR3: Key Topics Overview (150-200 words)
**Kinematic Chains**: Humanoid robots feature complex kinematic chains with multiple degrees of freedom that require sophisticated mathematical models to describe their motion. Forward kinematics computes end-effector positions from joint angles, while inverse kinematics solves for joint angles to achieve desired end-effector positions.

**Dynamic Modeling**: Humanoid dynamics involve complex interactions between multiple links, actuators, and environmental forces. Dynamic models account for Coriolis forces, centripetal forces, and gravitational effects that influence robot motion and stability.

**Balance Control**: Maintaining balance in bipedal robots requires real-time control algorithms that adjust center of mass and foot placement. Techniques like Zero Moment Point (ZMP) and Linear Inverted Pendulum Model (LIPM) are essential for stable humanoid locomotion.
